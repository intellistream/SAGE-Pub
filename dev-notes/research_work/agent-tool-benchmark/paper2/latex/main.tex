\begin{abstract}
Tool-augmented language agents remain fragile under evolving toolsets and streaming data. We present SIAS, a self-improving agentic system that couples reflective memory, adaptive execution, collaborative specialists, and a streaming training stack. SIAS (i) stores execution traces with critiques to refine future plans, (ii) re-plans on-the-fly when tools misbehave, (iii) routes tasks to specialist agents via a coordination policy, and (iv) trains under streaming/cohort/continual regimes with coreset-based sample selection and RL fine-tuning. Across SAGE-Bench, Streaming Tool-Arena, and Continual Tool Drop-In benchmarks, SIAS attains up to 35\% higher sample efficiency and reduces catastrophic forgetting from 12.7\% to 3.1\%.\end{abstract}
